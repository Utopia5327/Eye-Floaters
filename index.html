Floaters

They drift. Try to look directly at one. Blink and the world shifts.

You have always had them.

Threads. Specks. A wisp of cobweb at the edge of vision. You've had them since childhood. You just stopped noticing.

One morning, I noticed mine again. I tried to look directly at one.

It moved away.

Floaters are fragments of tissue suspended in the vitreous — the gel that fills your eye. They drift slowly, suspended in fluid, casting faint shadows on the retina.

Try to fix your gaze on one and it evades you. Your eye moves toward it. The floater moves with it. The fovea never arrives.

You can only see them by not looking.

I built this with Claude — eye tracking, fluid physics, the exact mechanics of the slip. I knew what I'd been watching. Claude knew the math.

— Manas Bhatia, 2026
